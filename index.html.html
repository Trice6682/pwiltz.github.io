Introducing Medical Technologists

A medical technologist (MTs) is a healthcare professional who performs sample testing on blood, bodily fluids, and
tissues to aid in patient diagnosis, treatment, and disease prevention. MTs operate a variety of laboratory equipment
and analyzers, follow strict regulatory protocols, and interpret data with precision. Some MTs are considered
"generalists" which means they work every department in the lab; others are considered "specialists" in which they
only work one lab department. Laboratory departments include:hematology, microbiology, immunology, flow cyto
metry, blood bank, urinalysis, and clinical chemistry. Relatively, MTs must be able to troubleshoot equipment mal
functions, identify abnormalities in test results, and collaborate with healthcare professionals to ensure patient care.

Required MT Education

To become a Medical Technologist, one typically needs a bachelor's degree in medical laboratory science or clinical
laboratory science. This undergraduate program usually includes coursework in biology, chemistry, microbiology, and
other life sciences, along with hands-on laboratory training. After completing their education, aspiring medical techno-
logists often need to obtain certification. Certification requirements vary by state and employer but typically involve
passing a national certification exam offered by organizations such as the American Society for Clinical Pathology
(ASCP), the American Medical Technologists (AMT), or the National Credentialing Agency (NCA).
